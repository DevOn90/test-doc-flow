## Questions

### Question 1
**Which notification method do you generally prefer?**
| Option               | Responses |   % |
| -------------------- | --------: | --: |
| Email                |        15 | 54% |
| In-app notification  |         8 | 29% |
| No strong preference |         5 | 18% |

---

### Question 2
**Why do you prefer that option?**

**Email**
- I check email throughout the workday.
- Easier to find important information later.
- Less likely to miss than in-app messages.

**In-app**
- I ignore most emails.
- Notifications are relevant only while using the app.
- I dislike inbox clutter.

**No preference**
- As long as it's timely.
- Depends on urgency.
- Either is fine.

## Question 3

**Have you ever missed an important notification because it arrived through the wrong channel?**

| Answer | Responses |
| ------ | --------: |
| Yes    |        18 |
| No     |        10 |

Interesting observation:<br>
Many respondents said they missed emails because they arrived too late or were buried among newsletters.

## Question 4

**How important is timely delivery compared to notification channel?**

| Option                        | Responses |
| ----------------------------- | --------: |
| Timing is much more important |        17 |
| Both equally important        |         8 |
| Channel much more important   |         3 |

---

## Question 5

**Would you prefer another notification method?**

Most common answers:
- Push notifications
- SMS for urgent alerts
- Ability to configure notification preferences

---

## Representative Quotes

> "Email is great for records, but I don't notice it immediately."

> "If I'm already in the app, don't send me an email."

> "I just want important things to arrive quickly."

> "Give me the option to choose."

---

## Findings

### Finding 1
Email is the preferred notification channel for a slight majority (54%).

### Finding 2
Notification timing appears to be more important than notification channel.

### Finding 3
Users are not homogeneous.

Different users clearly prefer different channels.
